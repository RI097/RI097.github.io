Overview

Problem

Art galleries are a lovely place to hangout.After covid and galleries reopening,the process needs to be streamlined.An added touch could be enriching the experience and adding a bit of nuance to it.





Solution

I designed Vera,a mobile app that enables our users to seamlessly go through the entry process of the art gallery and add a touch to their art viewing experience.It allows users to view details about the art piece they are seeing through audio tours and even get a feel of that era through the music matching with the theme of the art.Other additional details include gallery maps and an explore section where other activities at the art gallery are mentioned

Foundational Research

When I first started with this project,I laid down some research goals and understood the span of my target audience before becoming specific with user personas.

    Research goals

  • Understand the needs of people visiting the art gallery
  • Understand why do people visit the place and do they enjoy the experience or could it be improved

    Characteristics of your target audience

  • People who enjoy art and visit galleries and museums often
  • Include participants of different age groups
  • Include participants with disabilities


I did not go through the interview process due to non availability of participants.I have some interview questions which helped me better. Additionally,I worked on the 5 W's framework.





    Interview questions

  • Why do you visit art galleries or museums?
  • What do you do once you reach an art gallery?
  • What do you see at the gallery or do you cover each artwork completely?
  • Do you feel that your experience could be enriched?
  • How often do you visit these places?
  • How invested are you in art?
  • What do you see these places as?

5 Ws framework

    Who is experiencing the problem?

  • By users who visit the art gallery or who plan to visit
  • What is the problem?
  • They need to wait in queues to book the tickets
  • The gallery is too large that sometimes they get lost or cannot navigate through it smoothly
  • They want to know more about the artworks on display and the artist

    Where does the problem show up?

  • The problem shows up in the art gallery

    When does the problem occur?

  • It occurs when a person looks at the painting and is unable to find the artist or know more about his work
  • When they enter the gallery and get confused on where to start
  • When they think that visiting the gallery could be something more than just viewing the work
  • When they don’t know the schedule of the art gallery

    Why is the problem important?

  • The person doesn't visit the gallery frequently to understand the layout and remember it
  • The person needs information for their personal or professional interests regarding the art
  • The person could save time by booking a ticket beforehand




I have focussed on four pain points and four goals.
I have drawn out their user journeys to understand that better.The process was eye opener.It showed me what each of the user persona was going through and on the way I came across some roadblocks they faced.This helped my research.

User Research study / Design Research

A basic study script was created to check if the prototype is easy to use

    KPIs were

  1. Time on task -How much time does it take to book a slot to visit the gallery?
  2. Conversion rates -How many users are able to book tickets and access the audio tours?
  3. User error rates -How often do the users get stuck or make errors while using the app?

    Overview of the script

  • Introduce the project goal, and put users at ease.
  • Describe the scenario, set up the context, and let users use the app while thinking aloud.
  • Ask follow up questions and gather feedback.
  • Let users fill out a questionnaire. Thank them for participating.
